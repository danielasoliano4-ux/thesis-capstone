Rabies First Aid & Emergency Guide

Immediate steps to take after an animal bite. Act fast — every minute matters in rabies prevention.

CRITICAL WARNING

Rabies is 100% fatal once symptoms appear. However, it is 100% preventable when treated promptly after exposure. Do not wait — seek medical attention at an Animal Bite Treatment Center (ABTC) immediately after any animal bite.

Immediate First Aid Steps
1

Wash the wound immediately

Use soap and running water vigorously for at least 15 minutes. This is the single most effective step in reducing the risk of rabies infection. Wash all scratches, licks on broken skin, and bites thoroughly.

2

Apply an antiseptic solution

After washing, apply povidone-iodine (Betadine), 70% alcohol, or hydrogen peroxide to the wound. These disinfectants help kill the rabies virus on the wound surface.

3

Do NOT cover or suture the wound

Leave the wound open to air whenever possible. Covering or suturing the wound traps the virus and increases the risk of infection. Only use loose dressing if necessary to control bleeding.

4

Go to the nearest ABTC immediately

Proceed to an Animal Bite Treatment Center (ABTC) or clinic within 24 hours of the bite. Do not wait for symptoms to appear — by then it is too late. Use this app to find the nearest clinic with anti-rabies vaccines available.

5

Complete the full vaccination schedule

Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) requires multiple doses over several days. Complete ALL scheduled doses even if you feel fine. Missing doses significantly reduces protection.

6

Report the animal

Report the animal that bit you to your local barangay or the City Veterinary Office. If possible, observe the animal for 10 days. A healthy animal after 10 days generally means you were not exposed to rabies.

WHO Exposure Categories

Understanding Your Exposure Level

The World Health Organization classifies animal bite exposures into three categories. Your doctor will use this classification to determine the appropriate treatment.

Category Type of Contact Required Action
Category I Touching or feeding of animals; licks on intact skin Wash exposed skin; no vaccination required if history is reliable
Category II Minor scratches or abrasions without bleeding; nibbling of uncovered skin; licks on broken skin Wound washing + immediate vaccination
Category III Single or multiple transdermal bites or scratches; contamination of mucous membranes; contact with bats Wound washing + rabies immunoglobulin (RIG) + immediate vaccination
Important Information

Available Anti-Rabies Vaccines

  • Verorab — Vero cell rabies vaccine (Sanofi)
  • Rabipur — Purified chick embryo cell vaccine (GSK)
  • Speeda — MassBiologics vaccine
  • All vaccines are safe and highly effective when given promptly

Vaccination Schedule (PEP)

  • Day 0 — First dose (as soon as possible after bite)
  • Day 3 — Second dose
  • Day 7 — Third dose
  • Day 14 — Fourth dose
  • Day 28 — Fifth (final) dose

What NOT to Do

  • Do NOT apply herbs, mud, or leaves to the wound
  • Do NOT suck out the wound
  • Do NOT delay seeking medical attention
  • Do NOT skip vaccination doses
  • Do NOT ignore minor scratches from animals

Symptoms of Rabies

  • Fever, headache, general weakness (early)
  • Anxiety, confusion, agitation
  • Fear of water (hydrophobia)
  • Fear of air (aerophobia)
  • Hallucinations and paralysis (late — fatal)
